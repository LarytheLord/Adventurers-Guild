# Product Hunt Execution Checklist

Use this as the operational checklist for the Adventurers Guild Product Hunt launch.

## Decision

Recommended path:

1. Do not launch immediately.
2. Ship Copilot Hints MVP first.
3. Start a build-in-public thread around the new positioning.
4. Collect early user reactions.
5. Launch on Product Hunt 2 to 4 weeks later.

Reason: Product Hunt rewards products with a clear story, visible proof, and founder momentum. Launching after the copilot MVP gives you a stronger story than launching only the marketplace.

## Positioning

Primary message:

**Learn to code, earn money, and rank up through real coding quests.**

Secondary message:

**Adventurers Guild is a learn-while-you-earn platform where developers complete real coding quests, earn rewards, gain XP, and get help from a quest copilot.**

Avoid positioning it as:

- Just another freelance marketplace.
- Just another coding course.
- Just another AI coding assistant.

## 4-Week Launch Timeline

### Week 1: Product Proof

- [ ] Ship Copilot Hints MVP or have a polished demo.
- [ ] Add a landing page section explaining “learn while you earn.”
- [ ] Make the quest detail page screenshot-worthy.
- [ ] Create a demo user/account if needed.
- [ ] Confirm the signup flow works on mobile.
- [ ] Verify the main CTA is clear.

### Week 2: Story And Social Proof

- [ ] Get 3 adventurer testimonials.
- [ ] Get 1 company/client testimonial if possible.
- [ ] Record a 45 to 60 second demo video.
- [ ] Create 5 to 7 product screenshots.
- [ ] Write the Product Hunt title, tagline, and description.
- [ ] Draft the founder comment.
- [ ] Draft the Twitter/X thread.
- [ ] Draft the LinkedIn launch post.

### Week 3: Community Warm-Up

- [ ] Make a list of 50 people who might support the launch.
- [ ] Warm up with founder communities, not with spam.
- [ ] Post 1 build-in-public thread about the copilot decision.
- [ ] Post 1 founder story thread about why this matters.
- [ ] Comment on 5 to 10 Product Hunt launches genuinely.
- [ ] Ask 5 trusted people to review the Product Hunt copy.

### Week 4: Launch Prep

- [ ] Finalize all screenshots.
- [ ] Finalize demo video.
- [ ] Finalize Product Hunt page.
- [ ] Schedule launch for Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday at 12:01 AM PT.
- [ ] Prepare launch-day comment sequence.
- [ ] Prepare email to existing users.
- [ ] Prepare DMs to supporters.
- [ ] Block launch day on calendar.

## Launch Day Timeline

### 12:01 AM PT

- [ ] Publish Product Hunt page.
- [ ] Post founder comment immediately.
- [ ] Post Twitter/X launch thread.
- [ ] Post LinkedIn launch post.
- [ ] Send email to users.
- [ ] Send DMs to closest supporters.

### First 2 Hours

- [ ] Reply to every Product Hunt comment.
- [ ] Thank early supporters.
- [ ] Share the link in 2 to 3 relevant communities.
- [ ] Do not spam unrelated communities.

### Morning PT

- [ ] Post follow-up comment about the copilot.
- [ ] Reply to every comment again.
- [ ] Share early momentum update if ranking well.
- [ ] Ask for feedback, not just upvotes.

### Afternoon PT

- [ ] Post follow-up comment about traction and vision.
- [ ] Share the founder story if the discussion is active.
- [ ] DM founders, VCs, bootcamp people with a feedback ask.

### Evening PT

- [ ] Share final push update.
- [ ] Reply to all comments before the day ends.
- [ ] Screenshot ranking, comments, and testimonials.
- [ ] Save useful comments for future marketing.

## Content Order

Do this before Product Hunt:

1. Build-in-public thread: why we are adding a copilot.
2. Founder story post: why junior developers need learn-while-you-earn.
3. Product demo post: quest page with copilot hints.
4. Product Hunt launch announcement.

This creates a space around the idea before the launch.

## Build-In-Public Thread

Use this 7 to 14 days before launch.

```text
1/ At a founder meet, I got a piece of feedback that changed how I think about Adventurers Guild.

We should not only provide work to developers.
We should help them learn and move forward while doing the work.

2/ That matters because junior developers often get stuck between tutorials and real projects.

Tutorials are safe but do not create enough real-world proof.
Freelance work is real but can be overwhelming without guidance.

3/ So we are adding a quest copilot.

The first version is simple: a sidebar inside quests with getting-started hints, common mistakes, implementation patterns, and resources.

4/ The goal is not to give developers the answer.

The goal is to help them think clearly, get unstuck, and finish real work with more confidence.

5/ Long term, I think developer education should move closer to real work.

Learn through quests.
Earn through completion.
Rank up through verified progress.

6/ This changes Adventurers Guild from only a marketplace to a learn-while-you-earn platform.

That is the direction we are building toward.
```

## Founder Story Thread

Use this 3 to 7 days before launch.

```text
1/ I am 19 and building my first startup.

I do not come from a big network where tech opportunities are easy to access.

That is one reason I care so much about Adventurers Guild.

2/ A lot of new developers do not lack ambition.
They lack a path.

They need real work, feedback, confidence, and a way to prove themselves.

3/ Courses can teach the basics.
Freelance platforms can provide work.

But there is a gap between learning and earning.

4/ Adventurers Guild is my attempt to fill that gap.

Developers complete real coding quests, earn rewards, gain XP, and rank up as they improve.

5/ Now we are adding a copilot inside quests so new adventurers do not feel alone when they get stuck.

This is the direction I believe in: learn while you earn.
```

## Product Hunt Page Final Copy

Recommended title:

**Adventurers Guild**

Recommended tagline:

**Learn to code, earn money, and rank up through real quests.**

Recommended description:

```text
Adventurers Guild is a gamified learn-while-you-earn platform for developers.

Adventurers complete real coding quests from companies, earn rewards, gain XP, build verified work history, and rank up from F to S.

We are adding a quest copilot that helps new developers get unstuck with contextual hints, common mistakes, implementation patterns, and resources directly inside the quest page.

The goal is to help developers learn through real work while giving companies access to talent with visible progression and proof of execution.
```

## Things To Avoid

- [ ] Do not launch before the copilot MVP is demoable.
- [ ] Do not say “AI-powered” without showing where the AI helps.
- [ ] Do not make the description too broad.
- [ ] Do not ask strangers only for upvotes.
- [ ] Do not disappear after launch.
- [ ] Do not ignore critical feedback.
- [ ] Do not launch on Friday or weekend.
- [ ] Do not use fake traction numbers.

## Success Targets

Minimum:

- 100 Product Hunt upvotes.
- 20 comments.
- 50 new signups.
- 3 useful investor/founder conversations.

Strong:

- Top 10 of the day.
- 200 upvotes.
- 50 comments.
- 100 new signups.
- 1 bootcamp conversation.

Excellent:

- Top 5 of the day.
- 300+ upvotes.
- 100+ comments.
- 250+ new signups.
- 3 investor conversations.
- 2 bootcamp partnership leads.

## Final Rule

Start with the thread. Build the space around the idea. Then launch on Product Hunt.

The Product Hunt launch should be the peak of the story, not the beginning of it.
