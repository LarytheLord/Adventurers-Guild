# Product Hunt Asset Kit: Adventurers Guild

Use this when you are ready to prepare the actual Product Hunt page, Twitter/X thread, LinkedIn post, email outreach, and launch-day comments.

## Recommended Launch Positioning

Do not launch as only a freelance marketplace.

Launch as:

**A gamified learn-while-you-earn platform where developers complete real coding quests, earn money, rank up, and get guided by an AI copilot.**

This is stronger because it creates a bigger story:

- Marketplace: companies post real work.
- Learning: adventurers improve through guided quests.
- AI copilot: reduces beginner friction and increases completion.
- Ranking: creates trust and progression.
- Bootcamp angle: partners can use this as real-world capstone infrastructure.

## Launch Timing Recommendation

Do not launch on Product Hunt immediately.

Use this order:

1. Ship the Copilot Hints MVP.
2. Test with 10 to 20 adventurers.
3. Collect 3 testimonials and 2 product screenshots.
4. Publish a founder/build-in-public thread.
5. Engage with Product Hunt and maker communities for 10 to 14 days.
6. Launch on Product Hunt.

Best launch window: 2 to 4 weeks after the Copilot Hints MVP is usable.

### Short Description

Adventurers Guild is a gamified developer platform where adventurers complete real coding quests for companies, earn money, gain XP, climb ranks from F to S, and get guided by an AI copilot that helps them learn while doing real work.

### Longer Description

Junior developers often face a bad tradeoff: learn through tutorials without income, or try freelancing without guidance and get stuck.

Adventurers Guild combines both sides. Developers complete real coding quests from companies, earn rewards, gain XP, build a public track record, and rank up from F to S.

We are now adding an AI copilot on quest pages so new adventurers get contextual hints, common mistakes, recommended patterns, and resources while they work. The goal is not to give away answers. The goal is to help developers learn faster and finish real work with confidence.

For companies, this means access to developers with visible progression and verified work history. For bootcamps, this can become real-world capstone infrastructure where students learn, ship, and earn.

## FAQ Answers For Launch Day

### How do developers get paid?

Companies post quests with rewards. Adventurers apply or accept, complete the work, and payments are processed after approval.

### Is this only for beginners?

No. The rank system goes from F to S. Beginners can start with easier quests, while advanced developers can unlock harder, higher-value work.

### How is this different from Upwork?

Upwork is mostly a freelance marketplace. Adventurers Guild is built around progression, learning, XP, rank, and verified quest history.

### How is this different from LeetCode?

LeetCode is practice. Adventurers Guild is real work. Developers build portfolio-worthy projects, earn rewards, and get evaluated on actual output.

### What does the AI copilot do?

The first version gives contextual hints, common mistakes, patterns, and resources inside quests. Later versions will support conversational help and adaptive learning paths.

### Does the AI give away solutions?

That is not the goal. The copilot should guide and unblock, not replace the developer’s work.

### Who pays?

The developer side should stay low-friction. The main business model is company-side monetization, payment fees, and later bootcamp/learning partnerships.

## Launch Day Rule

Do not ask only for upvotes.

Ask for feedback.

Better:

```text
Would love your feedback on the positioning and product.
```

Avoid:

```text
Please upvote us.
```

Product Hunt culture rewards authentic maker energy, not spam.

## Final Recommendation

Best path:

1. Ship Copilot Hints MVP.
2. Start a build-in-public thread about why you are adding it.
3. Collect early user reactions.
4. Build a small audience around “learn while you earn for developers.”
5. Launch on Product Hunt after the story has proof.

The thread comes before the Product Hunt launch. The Product Hunt launch should feel like the moment the story becomes public, not the first time anyone hears about it.
